Key Insights & Achievements:

Performed Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on 8,800+ Netflix titles to identify global content trends using Pandas, Matplotlib, and NumPy.

Visualized content distribution — found that Movies account for ~70% of all Netflix content, while TV Shows represent ~30%.

Identified Top Contributing Countries: The United States, India, and the United Kingdom emerged as the top three content-producing countries on Netflix.

Analyzed Year-wise Additions: Observed a steady increase in content between 2015–2021, peaking in 2020 due to the streaming boom during the pandemic.

Genre Insights: Discovered that the most common genres include International Movies, Dramas, and Comedies, showing Netflix’s focus on diverse global audiences.

Top Directors Identified: Highlighted the most frequent directors on the platform, including prominent figures contributing to multiple titles.

Country-Specific Analysis: Found that India contributes around 5–6% of Netflix’s total titles, showcasing its growing regional content presence.

Release Year Trend: Showed a sharp rise in titles released after 2015, aligning with Netflix’s aggressive global expansion strategy.

Data Cleaning & Transformation: Handled missing data, extracted the year from the “date_added” column, and created new features to analyze yearly trends.

Business Impact Insight: The analysis indicates that Netflix’s strategy of diversifying by geography and genre has successfully increased platform engagement.
